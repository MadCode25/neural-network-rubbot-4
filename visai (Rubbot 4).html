What is this AI brain "stress"?

AI brain stress determines how “strained” the neural network is while processing information. It is calculated from how extreme the hidden neuron activations are. When activations stay near a balanced state (around 0.5), stress is low. When activations move toward extremes (0 or 1), stress increases, meaning the AI is making more rigid or overconfident decisions. High stress can indicate confusion, saturation, or limited flexibility in the network’s thinking process.

How do I reduce it?

To reduce AI brain stress, it is recommended to balance the number of hidden neurons and improve training conditions such as epoch count and data quality. The appropriate balance depends on the size of the network and how long it is trained (epochs). In general, increasing neuron capacity allows the model to distribute workload more effectively, while proper training duration ensures stable learning without overloading the system.
